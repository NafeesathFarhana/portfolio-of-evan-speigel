Evan Spiegel

Cofounder, Snap

About Evan speigel

  • Evan Spiegel, CEO of Snap, became a billionaire at the age of 25.
  • Each day some 293 million people use Snapchat to send disappearing photos and videos.
  • Spiegel launched the company with Bobby Murphy, a Stanford University fraternity brother, in 2011.
  • Murphy and Spiegel jointly own about a quarter of Snap but have voting shares that give them control over the board.
  • He graduated from Stanford with a B.S. in product design in 2018, six years after he first dropped out to start Snapchat.
  • Spiegel has donated more than $280 million in Snap stock. He and Murphy have pledged to donate 13 million shares to the Snap Foundation.

Achievements

Co-founding Snapchat: In 2011, Evan Spiegel, along with Bobby Murphy and Reggie Brown, created Snapchat while they were students at Stanford University. The app gained attention for its unique concept of disappearing photos and messages, which quickly appealed to a younger demographic.
Expanding Snapchat's user base: Under Spiegel's leadership, Snapchat grew rapidly and attracted millions of users worldwide. The platform offered a different approach to social media, focusing on sharing moments that disappear after being viewed, emphasizing privacy and authenticity.
Introduction of Stories and innovative features: In 2013, Snapchat introduced the "Stories" feature, allowing users to compile photos and videos that would be available to their followers for 24 hours. This feature became immensely popular and was later adopted by other social media platforms. Spiegel's company continued to innovate by introducing augmented reality (AR) filters, geolocation-based features, and interactive lenses, among other features.
Going public: In March 2017, Snap Inc. went public with an initial public offering (IPO), making Evan Spiegel one of the youngest self-made billionaires. The IPO valued Snap Inc. at approximately $24 billion.

Projects

Snapchat: Evan Spiegel co-founded Snapchat in 2011 along with Bobby Murphy and Reggie Brown. Snapchat is a multimedia messaging app known for its disappearing messages and augmented reality features. Spiegel played a crucial role in the development and growth of Snapchat, leading the company as CEO

Global Expansion: Snapchat has made efforts to expand its user base globally by targeting markets outside of the United States. The app has been localized in several languages, and Snap Inc. has actively pursued partnerships and collaborations with international brands and influencers to engage users in different regions.

Snap Originals: Under Evan Spiegel's leadership, Snap Inc. has expanded its offerings beyond messaging with the introduction of Snap Originals. Snap Originals are original shows produced exclusively for Snapchat, featuring short-form content in various genres, including drama, comedy, and docuseries.

AR and Camera Development: Evan has been actively involved in the development of augmented reality (AR) and camera technologies at Snap Inc.Snapchat has been at the forefront of integrating AR into its platform,offering users a range of interactive AR lenses and filters.Evan has emphasized the importance of AR in shaping the future.
